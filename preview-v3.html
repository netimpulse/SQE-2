What makes SQE different from other solar consultants?
+

SQE is unique because all our experts are officially appointed and certified expert witnesses. We combine international experience with specialized expertise in corrosion protection and durability assessment. Our independence and impartiality ensure credible, legally-sound expert reports recognized across multiple jurisdictions.

In how many countries do you operate?
+

We have successfully completed projects in over 30 countries across Europe, Asia, Middle East, Africa, and Australia. Our team's multilingual capabilities and international certifications enable us to serve clients globally while maintaining the highest standards of professional practice.

What exactly is corrosion protection assessment?
+

Corrosion protection assessment evaluates the effectiveness of protective systems for solar installations in challenging environments. We analyze material compatibility, environmental factors, protective coatings, and grounding systems to ensure long-term durability and safety. This is especially critical for coastal and industrial installations.

How do you conduct thermal imaging analysis?
+

Our thermal imaging services use advanced infrared cameras to detect heat anomalies, potential defects, and performance issues in solar systems. We capture images under controlled conditions and provide detailed analysis identifying problem areas like faulty modules, bad connections, or installation defects.

What is the legal validity of your expert reports?
+

Our reports are prepared by publicly appointed and sworn expert witnesses, giving them full legal standing. They are accepted in courts, administrative proceedings, and regulatory bodies across multiple jurisdictions. Our findings carry the authority of official expertise and can be used as evidence in disputes or compliance audits.

How long does a typical expert report take?
+

Timelines vary depending on project scope and complexity. A standard PV assessment typically takes 2-4 weeks from initial consultation to final report. Complex installations or corrosion analysis may require additional time. We provide clear timelines at the initial consultation phase.

What happens during the on-site analysis phase?
+

During on-site visits, our experts conduct comprehensive inspections including visual assessment, thermal imaging, electrical testing, and measurement of protective systems. We document all findings with photographs and technical data. This phase typically takes 1-3 days depending on system size and complexity.

Do you provide ongoing support after the report?
+

Yes, we provide comprehensive follow-up support including clarification of findings, recommendations implementation guidance, and periodic re-assessment services. We're committed to long-term partnerships and ensuring our clients understand and act on our recommendations effectively.
